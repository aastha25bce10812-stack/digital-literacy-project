Stay Safe Online:

  1. Your PIN is for sending, never receiving
        If someone asks you to enter your UPI PIN or scan a
        QR code to get a refund, cashback, or prize — that's
        a scam. Period. Money comes to you automatically;
        no PIN needed.

  2. Always check the name before hitting "Pay"
        Before confirming any payment, verify the recipient's
        name shown on the app — not just the number. Fraudsters
        use numbers close to real contacts. Two seconds of
        checking can save thousands.

  3. Never trust a screenshot as payment proof
        Selling something online? Check your bank or UPI app
        for the credit notification — not the buyer's screenshot.
        Fake "Payment Successful" images are embarrassingly
        easy to make.

  4. OTP, PIN, CVV — not even for "bank officials"
        No real bank, app, or police officer will ever call
        and ask for your OTP or PIN. If someone does: hang up
        immediately. Sharing it even once can drain your account
        in seconds.

  5. Use different passwords for different accounts
        If your Instagram password is the same as your banking
        password — fix that today. Use a free password manager
        like Bitwarden to create and remember unique passwords
        for every account.


  6. Ignore unsolicited "KYC update" or "job offer" links
        Random links in SMS or WhatsApp claiming KYC expiry,
        courier delivery, or a Rs.50k/month job? They install
        spyware or steal your credentials. When in doubt —
        don't click.

  7. Lock your payment apps with biometrics
        Enable fingerprint or face lock on GPay, PhonePe, and
        Paytm — separate from your phone lock. If your phone is
        stolen and unlocked, your money stays protected.

  8. Keep your apps and OS updated
        Updates aren't just new features — they patch security
        holes that hackers actively exploit. Enable auto-updates
        on your phone so you're never running vulnerable software.


   IF SOMETHING GOES WRONG — ACT IN THE NEXT FEW MINUTES


  CALL 1930  —  National Cyber Crime Helpline
               Call immediately after a financial fraud.
               Speed matters; they can sometimes block the
               transfer before it settles.

  cybercrime.gov.in  —  National Cyber Crime Portal
               File a detailed complaint online. Also report
               fake profiles and scam calls via the Chakshu
               feature on the Sanchar Saathi portal.
